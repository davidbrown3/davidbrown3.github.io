Virtual Displacements

For a given system position of x(t)x(t)x(t), if a velocity x˙(t)\dot x(t)x˙(t) satisfies all the conditions of the constraints, it is is called a "possible velocity". There are infinitely many possible velocities.

Integrating this with time we obtain a "possible infinitesimal displacement".

δx(t)=x˙dt \delta x(t) = \dot xdt δx(t)=x˙dt

Ideal Constraints

(Also known as "D'Alembarts principle", "Basic Equation of Analytical Mechanics")
